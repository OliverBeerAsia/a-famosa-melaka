## Audio Direction & Specifications

**Version**: 2.0
**Last Updated**: January 2026
**Audio Vision**: *Renaissance meets Gamelan — the sound of cultural collision*

---

## 1. AUDIO PHILOSOPHY

Sound is **half the experience**. When a player closes their eyes, they should still feel Melaka — the humid air thick with spice and salt, the distant call to prayer mixing with church bells, the bustle of a hundred languages in the market.

### Core Audio Pillars

1. **Cultural Fusion**: Portuguese, Malay, Chinese, Arab, Indian sounds layered authentically
2. **Environmental Immersion**: Location-specific ambient soundscapes
3. **Temporal Awareness**: Audio changes meaningfully with time of day
4. **Emotional Resonance**: Music evokes *saudade* (Portuguese longing) and tropical melancholy

### Reference Listening
- **Chrono Cross OST** (Yasunori Mitsuda) — Cultural fusion, melancholy
- **Portuguese Fado** (Amália Rodrigues) — *Saudade*, longing
- **Traditional Gamelan** — Interlocking rhythms, metallic tones
- **Shovel Knight / Celeste OST** — Modern chiptune excellence
- **Baldur's Gate tavern music** — Renaissance period feel (musical mood reference, not visual style)

## 2. MUSIC SPECIFICATIONS

### Technical Requirements
```
Format: OGG Vorbis (primary), MP3 (fallback)
Sample Rate: 44.1kHz
Bit Depth: 16-bit
Channels: Stereo
Loop Points: Seamless (specify loop start/end in metadata)
Normalization: -14 LUFS (streaming standard)
```

### Style: Chiptune/Lo-Fi Renaissance-Gamelan Fusion

Imagine a **16-bit SNES soundchip** playing music that blends:
- Portuguese lute and viola da gamba melodies
- Malay gamelan percussion patterns
- Chinese pentatonic scales
- A pervasive sense of tropical humidity and colonial tension

## 4. AMBIENT SOUND DESIGN

### Layering System
Each location has **3-4 ambient layers** that play simultaneously and can be individually adjusted:

```
Layer 1: BASE (always playing, location-specific foundation)
Layer 2: TIME (changes with time of day)
Layer 3: WEATHER (optional, for rain/wind events)
Layer 4: EVENTS (triggered by gameplay, temporary)
```

### Volume Mixing
```
Music: -12dB (background, never overpowering)
Ambient Base: -18dB (constant foundation)
Ambient Time: -20dB (supporting layer)
SFX (interactions): -6dB (prominent when triggered)
Dialogue Blips: -8dB (clear but not harsh)
```

### Cross-Location Ambient: Distant Azan

**Filename**: `ambient-distant-azan.ogg`
```
Duration: 45 seconds, one-shot (triggered at prayer times)
Contents:
- Faint call to prayer from the kampung surau, mixing with church bells
- Audible from: kampung (moderate), rua-direita (faint), waterfront (faint)
- NOT audible from: a-famosa-gate, st-pauls-church (Portuguese-controlled spaces)
```

**Historical Context**: Melaka's population remained majority Muslim throughout the Portuguese period. Although the Portuguese destroyed the great mosque and suppressed public Islamic worship, the call to prayer continued quietly from surau (small prayer houses) in the kampung. The layering of a distant azan beneath church bells captures the sonic reality of a colonial port where faiths coexisted in tension — the Islamic majority living under Christian rule. This ambient element should be subtle and atmospheric, never intrusive.

- **Tools**: LMMS, FamiStudio, Deflemask (chiptune)
- **Style**: 16-bit era sound chip aesthetic
- **Reference tracks**: Provide to composer with this document

### For Ambient/SFX
- **Freesound.org**: Creative Commons recordings
- **Field recording**: If possible, record actual market, water, birds
- **Processing**: All sounds should be "dirtied" slightly — not too clean/digital

### Cultural Authenticity
- **Gamelan samples**: Use authentic recordings as basis
- **Language snippets**: Research correct period terms
- **Instrument accuracy**: Portuguese 1580, not modern guitar
